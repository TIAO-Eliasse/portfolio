01 / About

Bridging AI Research
& Real-World Impact

I am an M.Sc. student in Artificial Intelligence at AIMS (Stellenbosch University), proudly funded by the Google DeepMind Scholarship — one of Africa's most competitive AI fellowships. Trained as a Statistician and Economic Engineer at ISSEA-CEMAC in Cameroon, my research bridges rigorous quantitative methods with cutting-edge machine learning.

My work focuses on three interconnected themes: Explainable AI (XAI) for decision support, Natural Language Processing for multilingual low-resource settings, and Probabilistic Forecasting with interpretable uncertainty. I am co-author of a Camera-Ready paper published at SemEval-2026 (ACL Anthology), and co-author of four additional papers currently under review for IndabaX 2026 and IJCAI 2026, spanning Human-AI collaboration, early-warning systems for SME survival, flood forecasting, and continual learning on African datasets.

Originally from Burkina Faso, educated across West and Central Africa, now conducting frontier AI research in Cape Town — my journey reflects resilience, excellence, and a commitment to making AI work for the African continent and the world.

Data-Driven Lifelines: An AI and Econometric Early-Warning System for SME Survival in Cameroon

Konlack Lonlack Giscard, Eliasse Tiao

Proposes a data-driven early-warning system for SME survival integrating econometric inference, ensemble ML, and survival analysis. Using 10,393 firms from Cameroon's national enterprise census (2017–2025), CatBoost achieves AUC = 0.884, macro F1 = 0.798. RSF attains C-index = 0.687 with firm-level survival curve estimation. Deployed as an interactive three-module dashboard on 23,162 SMEs, enabling proactive targeting of at-risk firms — directly relevant to Cameroon's NDS30 strategy.

🏛️ IndabaX / IJCAI 2026 · Under Review UNDER REVIEW

Optimizing Human-AI Collaboration for SME Survival: A Cost-Aware and Uncertainty-Guided Routing Approach

Eliasse Tiao, Lionel Cedric Gohouede, Mahugnon Aime Loick Gohouede, Josué Romaric Edou, Vicent Mwanda

Introduces S3, a cost-sensitive Human-AI routing framework for SME failure prediction. Decomposes model uncertainty into aleatoric (borderline cases → data analyst) and epistemic (diverging trajectories → survival specialist) channels. Evaluated on 7,859 Cameroonian SMEs, S3 reduces expected costs by 13.0% over full automation, satisfies 6/9 demographic fairness criteria, and cuts undetected failures by 97% on automated cases.

🏛️ IndabaX / IJCAI 2026 · Under Review UNDER REVIEW

FloodTriage: A Decision-Theoretic Tri-Fold Deferral Policy for Human-AI Collaboration in Streamflow Forecasting

Lionel Cedric Gohouede, Mahugnon Aime Loick Gohouede, Eliasse Tiao, Josué Romaric Edou

FloodTriage combines a five-member LSTM deep ensemble with an XGBoost surrogate and human-in-the-loop protocol, governed by a Tri-Fold Routing Gateway decomposing deferral into epistemic, aleatoric, and Knightian uncertainty signals. Applied to the Ouémé River (Benin, West Africa): reduces flood miss rate from 0.483 → 0.399, improves KGE from 0.720 → 0.784, while deferring only 25.9% of timesteps.

🏛️ IndabaX / IJCAI 2026 · Under Review UNDER REVIEW

Optimiser Geometry and Catastrophic Forgetting: Comparative Study on a Real-World African Agricultural Dataset

Josué Romaric Edou, Eliasse Tiao, Mahugnon Aime Loick Gohouede, Lionel Cedric Gohouede

Controlled comparative study of Adam, Sparse Adam, Natural Gradient, and EWC for continual learning on Cassava Leaf Disease dataset (Uganda, 21,418 images). Sparse Adam (k=10%) achieves highest average accuracy (0.614 ± 0.004), outperforming standard Adam (0.506 ± 0.010). EWC near-eliminates forgetting (BWT = -0.040) at cost of lowest accuracy. Provides practical guidance for African agricultural AI systems.
