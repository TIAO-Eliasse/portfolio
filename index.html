01 / ABOUT

Bridging AI Research & Real-World Impact

I am an M.Sc. student in Artificial Intelligence at AIMS (Stellenbosch University), proudly funded by the Google DeepMind Scholarship — one of Africa's most competitive AI fellowships. Trained as a Statistician and Economic Engineer at ISSEA-CEMAC in Cameroon, my research bridges rigorous quantitative methods with cutting-edge machine learning.


My work focuses on three interconnected themes: Explainable AI (XAI) for decision support, Natural Language Processing for multilingual low-resource settings, and Probabilistic Forecasting with interpretable uncertainty. I am co-author of a Camera-Ready paper accepted at SemEval-2026 (ACL Anthology) on fine-grained polarization detection using transformer LLMs.


Originally from Burkina Faso, educated across West and Central Africa, now conducting frontier AI research in Cape Town — my journey reflects resilience, excellence, and a commitment to making AI work for the African continent and the world.

Probabilistic Forecasting of Electricity Consumption & Solar PV Production with Explainable Uncertainty Attribution

Supervisors: Dr. Sabrina Amrouche & Amal Nammouchi

Developing a unified probabilistic framework that jointly models electricity consumption and solar PV production across hourly, daily, and weekly horizons. The core innovation combines conformal prediction for calibrated intervals (P10/P50/P90) with SHAP-based attribution to explain drivers of uncertainty — directly translating model outputs into actionable operational decisions.

Quantile Regression (LightGBM)
Conformal Prediction
DeepVAR (multivariate DL)
SHAP Attribution
P10 / P50 / P90 intervals
Hourly · Daily · Weekly
